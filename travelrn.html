What is Travel Resource Network?

A position in travel nursing can be a very lucrative and rewarding career. A travel nurse is typically contracted to work at a specific location for a limited amount of time.

These contracts, otherwise known as assignments, usually last for 13-week periods and can often extend out to 52-week assignments, depending on the client's need.

Travel Resource Network aims to change that. The idea is a unified mobile application that only sources travel assignments for these clinicians.

Problem

Sourcing contract assignments can sometimes be problematic for travel nurses as they are usually navigating in and out of a handful of career websites. Not to mention the steps in creating accounts and filtering out "non-nursing positions" when looking for contract assignments - a process that can prove to be quite tedious and time consuming.

Solution

Travel Resource Network solves this problem by eliminating the redunancy found in creating multiple user accounts with various career applications.

As a mobile application dedicated to the travel nursing sector that is only meant for travel nurses - it introduces a one-stop-shop benefit for users to review locations where assignments are offered, preview pay packages, and obtain any other information relevant to the assignment, all while keeping the primary focus and goal for the end-user in the forefront.

User Surveys

Understanding Our Users

For the first step in this discovery phase, I needed to gather more information regarding my target audience, so I began with creating and distributing user surveys. Since this market was going to be a niche market, I solely targeted the nursing community.

With a former background in nurse recruitment services, I was able to leverage my contacts in this community who were more than willing to take my survey and pass it along to their colleagues.

At the conclusion of the survey, I tallied a total of 23 responses which were analyzed with the following results being documented:

  • 100% of the respondents used mobile applications to source information for professional and personal use daily
  • A little over half (52.2%) of the respondents used their mobile devices as an outlet to source their next travel assignment
  • Indeed, LinkedIn and Craigslist were the three top job sites used when looking for new travel assignments
  • All respondents (100%) collectively agreed that if a new application were to be introduced to the market whereby the product was solely intended for travel nurses, they would be interested in using the application
