ARCHETYPE is a cloud based storage and content management software for the creative user.

Modern design meets minimalist - the application offers a clean and simple experience for content creators to collect, organize, collaborate, create, and share their work with other creatives and fellow followers.

Problem

To date, there are several cloud storage platforms available to consumers, most of which try to cover a wide audience. What about a niche crowd of content creators?

How could a platform offer all in one services for organization, content creation, and collaboration?

Solution

ARCHETYPE aims to solve that problem by streamlining these services into one main platform. It will allow users to create and organize content, collaborate with other creatives, and share their work with their followers.

Market Research

The cloud storage space has been around for several years and is an established business, used by millions on a global scale.

Upon looking at the possibility of a new market entrant, I needed to first identify who were our competitors and understand the market. A SWOT Analysis was used to help initiate the process.

Competitive Analysis

Google Drive, Microsoft One Drive, and DropBox were targeted as key competitors in the market. Through the SWOT Analysis, I was able to identify certain weaknesses and opportunities for a new market entrant.

  • Google Drive has a strong presence in the market through its already existing userbase of other services. However, the features are basic and rather limited.
  • MS One Drive has a very strong UI with a clean interface. However, features and services are targeting the enterprise and family spaces.
  • DropBox has been around for quite sometime however, most of their market targeting are more torwards the enterprise and business sector.

User Surveys

90% are familiar with cloud storage platforms and currently use their services.

72% enjoyed basic features that were offered among all competing platforms.

79% felt web-saving capabilities, organization, content creation, and collaboration were the most beneficial of using these services.

Key Insights

The survey data revealed that the market was well versed in the uses of cloud storage platforms. Many subscribers engaged these platforms for professional and personal uses - content creation and collaboration.

And although subscription fees were listed as one of the frustrations, other issues like security features, user experience for first time users, and accessibility / inconsistencies of the service from one device to another were the main pain-points illustrated from the survey results.

User Personas

Using the data gained from the surveys, user personas were then created to assist with identifying the most common and basic functions needed from a new cloud storage platform.

The primary goal was to grow an understanding of potential users' adoption of a new service. And in order to address this, two user personas were created - the Visual Creator and the Content Collaborator.

User Stories

With the information given from the personas alongside the data generated from the surveys, the next step was to list out the basic features users would need. These features were outlined using user stories and ranked of importance from high to low frequencies.

  • Create a new account
  • Upload pictures and videos
  • Create notes and tags
  • Save a weblink
  • Upgrade account

Wireframes and Prototypes

Referencing the user flows as a guide, I then began sketching out the basic wireframes that would serve as a visual aid of how the application could operate.

Once the sketches were complete, I then used Figma to replicate the sketches and turned them into a Lo-Fi clickable prototype for user testing.

VIEW LO-FI PROTOTYPE

Lo-Fi Testing

After the intial wireframes were finished, I then imported them to InVision where they were put together to construct a Lo-Fi clickable prototype. User testing was conducted both in-person and remote through FaceTime. Feedback was provided during this period which later led to the following refinements.

  • Fix alignment of call to action buttons
  • Buttons need to be easily presentable to the user (i.e add item button seemed out of place to the user)
  • Fix typography for layout in the user dashboard field
  • Address spacing of buttons and typography throughout entire layout of design

Branding & Logo

Given that this industry was already a heavily saturated market, I had to identify a point of entry to make a splash large enough for consumers to notice. In order to achieve this, the product's branding needed to evoke a sense of purpose that also resonated with the brand's services - something to stoke the interest of the community.

Through the help of some mind mapping techniques and several sketches of what the product should convey, ARCHETYPE stood out with the most potential of what the brand would stand for.

The meaning behind ARCHETYPE is better expressed as the original pattern or model of which all things of the same kind are a representation of the original source. It will stand for ideas that are attractive yet simple with a creative personality and can be accessible to any content creator.

For the logo portion, I knew I wanted to present something that was synonymous of what ARCHETYPE stood for.

I set out to work pouring through numerous sketches of what a potential logo could be, but none seemed to have captured my attention. So rather than focusing on a mark, I redirected my focus on the name itself - a word that is hardly used or heard in everyday conversation and is unique enough to evoke a sense of curiousity of what the product was and the brand stood for. And after several iterations later, a final concept stood out to be the winning design.

Typography

Two typefaces were chosen for this brand that were intended to represent a strong charisma that is associated with the mood of what defines a brand.

Brand Typeface

Montserrat is the primary typeface of ARCHETYPE which is mainly used for headlines and displays. The typeface comes in a number of variations in weights and sizes.

Complimentary Typeface

Lato the complimentary typeface of ARCHETYPE which is mainly used for headlines and displays. The typeface comes in a number of variations in weights and sizes.

Colors

Three layers for the color palette were chosen, each representing a certain identity that would be expressed throughout the brand's overall look and character.

  • The primary color is turqouise which is meant to evoke a sense of professionalism and creativity and is also a great compliment to the various shades of gray and white colors.
  • Black illustrates the strength of the brand and can be seen in text, headings, and throughout the background.
  • Vermillion red adds vibrance and determination, while mustard yellow brings with it a splash of clarity and energy.
